Adding reverb and other audio effects

You can add reverb, echo, distortion, and other audio effects to manipulate the sound in your movie.

To add an audio effect:

  1. Click the Timeline Viewer button (shown above).
  2. Select the audio clip or clips you want to add an effect to.

    3. Tip: To learn how to select multiple clips, see "Selecting clips" below.

  3. Click the Editing button, and then click Audio FX at the top of the Editing pane.
  4. Select the audio effect you want from the effects list.
  5. Adjust the settings for the effect.

    6. Different controls appear depending on the effect you select. For example, if you select Reverb, you can adjust the intensity of the reverberation by choosing a reverb style and dragging the Less/More slider.

  6. Click Preview to hear the effect.

    7. To stop previewing the effect, click Preview again.

  7. If necessary, repeat any of the above steps until you have the effect the way you want it.
  8. To add the effect to your selected clips, click Apply.

When you add an audio effect to a video clip, iMovie automatically extracts the audio from the clip. The extracted audio with the applied effect appears as a separate clip in one of the audio tracks in the timeline viewer. If you want to add multiple audio effects to the same portion of video, be sure to select the extracted audio clip to add the additional effects to.

You can also add sound effects to your movie, such as an alarm, a dog's bark, the sound of rain, and more. To learn how, see Related Topics below.
