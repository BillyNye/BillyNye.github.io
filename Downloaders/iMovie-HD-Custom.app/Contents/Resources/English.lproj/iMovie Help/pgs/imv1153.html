Precisely aligning audio to video

You can use "snap lines" to precisely align audio clips to frames in your video. When the snap lines of an audio clip and video clip align, you'll have a very close fit.

To use snap lines:

Drag audio and video clips in the timeline viewer.

A yellow snap line appears when you reach the beginning or end of audio and video clips, the playhead, a bookmark, or a chapter marker.

You can turn off snap lines so that they appear only when you hold down the Shift key while dragging clips. To turn off snap lines, choose iMovie HD > Preferences, click General, and deselect the "Snap to items in Timeline" checkbox.

When snap lines are turned on, you can hold down the Shift key to turn them off when you need to.

You can also use snap lines and audio waveforms together to align your video frames to the audio. When waveforms are turned on, a snap line appears when you come within a few frames of audio silence.
