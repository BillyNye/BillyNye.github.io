Selecting part of a video clip

You can select part of a clip in the Clips pane, clip viewer, or timeline viewer. This is useful if you want to crop a clip, move a portion of it by copying and pasting, or add an effect to a specific part of the clip.

To select part of a clip:

  1. Select the clip by clicking it in the Clips pane, clip viewer, or timeline viewer.
  2. Drag the playhead (A, shown above) to where you want your selection to begin.
  3. Place your pointer (B, shown above) just below the playhead, and then drag to the right to include the footage you want to select.

    4. The gold portion of the scrubber bar (shown below) highlights the footage you've selected.

  4. To precisely adjust the length of your selection, click a crop marker (shown below) to select it, and then press the Left or Right Arrow key to move the crop marker one frame at a time. To move the marker in ten-frame increments, hold down the Shift key while pressing the arrow key.

You can also select a segment of video that extends over multiple clips. Choose Edit > Select None, and then drag the crop markers to enclose any segment of video you like. This is useful if you want to apply an effect over a specific part of your movie.
