About rendering

The process of creating new elements (such as transitions, titles, or effects) in a movie is called "rendering." Rendering happens any time a video clip is modified, such as when you add a dissolve between scenes or scroll text over video. During the rendering process, iMovie HD makes the appropriate changes to each frame in a clip. As iMovie HD renders a clip, you'll see a small red line progress along the bottom of the clip.

If you apply effects or transitions to more than one selected clip, iMovie HD renders all the clips at once, which can save you a lot of work.

You can continue editing your movie while your change is rendered. Rendering takes from a few seconds to a few minutes to complete.
