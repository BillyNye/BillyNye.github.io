Choosing whether to break clips at scene breaks as you import video

As you import your video, iMovie HD can automatically break the video into short clips wherever there's a scene break (places where the camcorder was switched from standby to record). Breaking up your clips automatically makes it easy to edit and add transitions at specific points in a movie without having to first split a long movie into scenes.

Note: iMovie HD is preset to create a new clip wherever there's a scene break, but you can turn this feature on or off.

To turn automatic scene detection on or off:

  1. Choose iMovie HD > Preferences.
  2. Click Import.
  3. Select or deselect "Start a new clip at each scene break."

You can also limit the length of an imported scene when you have automatic scene detection turned on. To do so, select the "Limit scene length" checkbox and specify a maximum length in minutes. When a scene reaches the limit you've specified, iMovie automatically creates a new clip.

If automatic scene detection is turned on and your imported footage isn't being broken at scene breaks, make sure the date and time on your camera are set correctly.

Note: iMovie HD does not break up scenes shorter than 30 frames (about one second), so some short scenes may be merged with longer ones when imported.
