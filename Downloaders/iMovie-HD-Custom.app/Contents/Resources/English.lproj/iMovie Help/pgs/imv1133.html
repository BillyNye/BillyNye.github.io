Showing audio waveforms

Audio waveforms are a graphic representation of audio intensity. You can use waveforms to see where an audio clip builds in intensity and use these visual cues to align your video frames to the audio.

Note: While an individual audio file can have a number of channels, the waveforms in iMovie HD show the average audio intensity for all channels in one waveform.

To turn on waveforms:

Choose View > Show Audio Waveforms.

Note: Waveforms are faint when you're showing clip volume levels. To hide clip volume levels, deselect Show Clip Volume Levels in the View menu. (A selected item has a checkmark next to it; choose the item again to deselect it.)

To see more detail in the waveform, it's a good idea to zoom in on the timeline viewer. For more information, see Related Topics below.
