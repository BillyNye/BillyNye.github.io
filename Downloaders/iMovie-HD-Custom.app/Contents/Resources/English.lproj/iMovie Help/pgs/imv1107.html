Adding photos from your iPhoto library

You can easily add photographs from your iPhoto library to use in a movie or to create a slideshow of favorite photographs.

To add a photo from your iPhoto library:

  1. Click the Media button, and then click Photos at the top of the Media pane.
  2. Choose your iPhoto library or any item within your library.

    3. Tip: You can also search for a photo by typing text in the search field. As you type, photos that contain the text you entered appear in the Photos pane. To see all photos again, click the Reset button (an "x" in the search field).

  3. Select the photo you want to add to your movie.
  4. In the Photo Settings window, drag the duration control (shown below) to change the display duration for your photo.
  5. If you want to pan across your photo, or zoom in or out on it, you can apply the Ken Burns Effect to it. To learn how, see "Adding motion to a photo" below.
  6. To add the photo to your movie, drag it from the Images pane to the clip viewer or timeline viewer.

You can also add multiple photos to your movie at the same time by selecting more than one photo in step 3 above. This is useful when building a slideshow of your photos. To learn how to select multiple photos, see "Selecting clips" below.

After you add a photo to your movie, you can change its display duration, crop it, and add motion to it at any time. For more information, see Related Topics below.
