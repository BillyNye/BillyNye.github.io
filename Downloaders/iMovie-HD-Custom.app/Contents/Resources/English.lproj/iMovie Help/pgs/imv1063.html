Selecting clips

In addition to selecting a single audio or video clip, you can select multiple clips to perform the same action to more than one clip at a time. For example, if you want to lock all the audio clips in your movie, you can select them all and then choose the "Lock Audio Clip at Playhead" command, instead of applying the command to each clip individually.

Selected clips are highlighted in blue:

Here are ways to select clips:

  • To select a clip, click it.
  • To select all the clips in the Clips pane, clip viewer, timeline viewer, or an audio track, select a clip and choose Edit > Select All.
  • To select a range of video clips in the Clips pane, clip viewer, or timeline viewer, hold down the Shift key as you select the first and last clips in the range you want.
  • To select several video clips located together in the Clips pane, clip viewer, or timeline viewer, click outside the clips and drag to enclose them within the selection rectangle.
  • To select clips that aren't adjacent to each other, hold down the Command key as you select the clips you want.
  • To select all the clips of a certain type, such as all the transitions in a movie, select a clip of the type you want to change and choose Edit > Select Similar Clips.
  • To deselect specific clips in a group of selected clips, hold down the Command key and click the clips you want to deselect.
  • To deselect all clips, click outside a clip.
