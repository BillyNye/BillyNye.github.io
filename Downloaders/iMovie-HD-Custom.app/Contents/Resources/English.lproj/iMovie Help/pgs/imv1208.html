Adjusting audio equalization

You can use the equalizer to change the levels of individual audio frequencies. This is useful if you want to adjust the bass or treble in an audio clip.

To adjust audio equalization:

  1. Click the Timeline Viewer button (shown above).
  2. Select the audio clip or clips whose sound you want to adjust.

    3. Tip: To learn how to select multiple clips, see "Selecting clips" below.

  3. Click the Editing button, and then click Audio FX at the top of the Editing pane.
  4. Click Graphic EQ in the effects list.
  5. Click Preview.
  6. To increase or decrease the bass, midrange, or treble, click a Preset button or drag the sliders up or down.
  7. To stop previewing the effect, click Preview again.
  8. If necessary, repeat any of the above steps until you have the effect the way you want it.
  9. To add the effect to your selected clips, click Apply.
